Rising Tides Competition (Pictured Above)

In order to encourage new ideas about how to design and build around a rising San Francisco Bay, the Bay Conservation and Development Commission (BCDC) sponsored an international design competition, in partnership with the American Institute of Architects, San Francisco Chapter, and with financial support from NOAA. The challenges to be addressed included: (1) rethinking how to build new communities in areas susceptible to future inundation; (2) retrofitting valuable public shoreline infrastructure; (3) protecting existing communities from flooding; (4) protecting wetlands; and (5) anticipating changing shoreline configurations. The environment to be addressed encompassed estuarine systems such as San Francisco Bay, Chesapeake Bay and the Yangtze River Basin.

More than 130 entries were received from around the world and thousands of people viewed them at the San Francisco Ferry Building. The six winning entries were chosen by an independent judging panel. Many singular and innovative ideas were represented by the diverse range of entries. The posters presenting the entries themselves became an important benefit from the competition.  The posters offer positive designs focused on how to respond to sea level rise. BCDC has curated the posters as a traveling exhibit in various public spaces to raise awareness about SLR and the need to adapt.
