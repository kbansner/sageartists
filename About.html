New Landscapes in Arts & Sciences


Great Art. Great Science. Each requires passion, hard work, skill, knowledge and imagination, which opens to us the world and universe, said Einstein, “all there ever will be to know and understand.”

To bring the disciplines and joys of art and science together - in the same room, on the same stage, and in our experience - is the driving force behind SAGE. When artists and scientists explore the reaches of the imagination beyond - but not excluding - the boundaries of convention, they enlighten us, they entertain us, and open a world filled with new ideas and experiences to the delight of us all.

SAGE Artists heralds such work.

Gail Schickele champions arts & sciences, taking her left and right brain wherever she travels.  

Her passion is inspired by the love, challenge and mystery of science as well as the arts, inspired by her artist father and first job in an uncle’s foreign film and mainstage theaters in Buffalo, New York, then later as marketing director for the historic Goldenrod Showboat on the St. Louis Mississippi riverfront; the Heritage Square Opera House in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains in Golden, Colorado; and Comedy Works in downtown Denver’s historic Larimer Square. 


As President of Creative Resource Network talent agency and later Spencer-DeFrancis Rublic Relations with business partner, singer-songwriter Kathi DeFrancis in the 80s and 90s in Denver, Gail worked with dozens of comedians, actors and musicians including Jay Leno, Roseanne Barr, Sam Kinison, Robin Williams, Louie Anderson, Bill Engvall, Jeff Foxworthy and Dennis Miller; represented talent and booked comedy clubs from coast to coast, presented or produced shows including Frank Ferrante’s one-man Groucho Marx, and with Bob Lacey the Rick & Ruby Rock n’ Roll Revival, and created, produced-or promoted  events ranging from Denver’s Bastille Day and Earth Day celebrations to G. William Oakley’s National Ragtime & Traditional Jazz Festivals on the St. Louis Mississippi riverfront.


She has canvassed and lobbied for the Ralph Nader-inspired Public Interest Research Group; worked as a writer-editor for the Sierra Club; served on the board of the Colorado Environmental Coalition of 30 member orgs;  taught a recycling program in Denver Public Schools; and wrote a play on nuclear arms produced as a college musical.


In California Gail has worked as a journalist for The Independent news in Livermore and as coordinator for downtown festivals sponsored by the newspaper. Environmental science inspires her educational and advocacy work nationally through work with Al Gore’s Climate Reality Project, and in California with groups such as the League of Women Voters, for which she’s worked for 20 years on various boards and committees at the state, regional and local levels as an activist, administrator and reporter on San Francisco Bay Area and California State environmental issues. She works with the Berkeley League Climate Team focusing on legislation and climate science outreach and co-chairs an environmental group founded by the late Elizabeth Schickele, a physicist whose shared her passion for science and art.


Gail was married to the late filmmaker-violist David Schickele and lives with their son in San Francisco.
